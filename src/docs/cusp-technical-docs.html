01 Protocol Overview

CUSP is the DeFi capital layer for prediction markets on Solana. The protocol makes prediction market positions productive by enabling yield, lending, borrowing, and leverage on outcome tokens - the tokenized YES/NO positions issued by DFlow representing Kalshi markets.

The protocol serves two sides: LPs who deposit USDC and earn yield from protocol activity, and traders who gain capital efficiency on high-conviction prediction market positions without closing their exposure.

Core thesis

Combined Kalshi and Polymarket open interest exceeds $800M. Every dollar of it earns 0%. CUSP is the protocol that makes it productive. We do not replace prediction markets - we make them financially complete.

What CUSP is not

  • Not a prediction market. We do not compete with Kalshi or Polymarket.
  • Not an oracle provider. We consume DFlow's price and resolution data.
  • Not a consumer trading interface. We are infrastructure for capital efficiency.

Reserve buffer rule

25% of all LP deposits are held as liquid USDC at all times. Never deployed. Never lent. This reserve covers LP withdrawals and serves as first-loss buffer if any strategy generates bad debt. The vault program enforces this programmatically - the allocate instruction rejects any deployment that breaches the 25% floor.

cUSDC exchange rate

cUSDC rate = Total Vault NAV / Total cUSDC Supply
NAV = USDC reserve + DFlow position values + Kamino deposits + outstanding loan principal + accrued interest - insurance allocation

The exchange rate only goes up as the vault earns. When an LP withdraws, they burn cUSDC and receive USDC at the current (higher) exchange rate. Withdrawals above 10% of TVL enter a 48-hour queue.

05 Liquidation Architecture

Binary outcome tokens are the hardest collateral type in DeFi to liquidate correctly. Standard lending protocols assume continuous price decline. YES/NO tokens jump instantly from $0.85 to $1.00 or $0.00 at resolution. CUSP handles this with three layered defense mechanisms.

The binary jump problem

A standard liquidation bot monitoring health factors every 60 seconds could see a perfectly healthy position at 11:58 PM and worthless collateral at midnight. No partial liquidation, no Dutch auction, no gradual price recovery is possible. The jump is instantaneous and discontinuous.

No existing lending protocol - Kamino, MarginFi, Aave - was designed to handle this. CUSP's liquidation architecture is purpose-built for this asset class.

Three lines of defense

Why full close, not partial liquidation

Kamino and MarginFi use partial liquidations - selling 10-20% of collateral to restore health. This works for continuous-price assets where partial sale can restore the collateral ratio. For binary outcome tokens, partial liquidation is dangerous: selling 20% of a deteriorating YES position does not change the binary outcome. If the market resolves NO, the remaining 80% goes to zero. Full close on breach is the only safe approach.

Critical user warning - show this on every borrow screen

Your loan is secured against the current market price of your token, not the final outcome. If the price drops before resolution, your position may be liquidated even if the market ultimately resolves in your favor. Your loan will be automatically closed at [timestamp] regardless of position health.

Show the exact liquidation price: "Your position will be liquidated if YES drops below $0.58. Current price: $0.80. Buffer: $0.22."

Every technical decision in CUSP is designed around DFlow's existing API surface. DFlow provides market access and routing into Kalshi without CUSP rebuilding exchange infrastructure. Outcome SPL tokens - the tokenized YES/NO positions - are the core asset primitive the entire protocol is built on.

Why outcome SPL tokens matter

DFlow issues real SPL tokens on Solana for every Kalshi market position. These are not synthetic wrappers or soft pegs - they are standard Solana tokens that can be transferred, deposited into programs, and used as collateral. Without this primitive, CUSP cannot exist. DFlow's Trade API returns ready-to-sign Solana transactions, which means CUSP's strategy engine never touches an order book, manages slippage, or handles market microstructure. This reduces engineering complexity by approximately 80%.

Circuit breaker conditions

The protocol automatically pauses new loan issuance (but not existing position monitoring) if any of these conditions are met:

  • Insurance fund below minimum threshold - protocol is absorbing unexpected losses
  • Total outstanding loans exceed 70% of pool TVL - over-leveraged system
  • DFlow API errors for 5+ consecutive minutes - cannot price collateral
  • Single market exceeds 15% of total collateral - concentration risk

Market isolation

Each DFlow market gets its own isolated risk parameters in the collateral registry. A failure in one market - unexpected early resolution, liquidity gap, Kalshi data delay - cannot cascade into adjacent positions. Bad debt from one market hits that market's reserve allocation first, then the insurance fund. It never touches LP capital deployed against unrelated markets.

Security measures

  • 48-hour upgrade timelock on all upgradeable programs - users can exit before changes take effect
  • 5-of-9 multisig for emergency pause - geographically distributed signers on Ledger hardware
  • Sherlock audit contest before mainnet launch
  • Immunefi bug bounty from day one - $200K max for critical findings
  • $1M TVL hard cap enforced in contract at launch, raised monthly if clean
